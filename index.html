The Artist

Storyteller. Spirit. Multipotentialite.

Bongani Givethanks (born Bongani Glen Sibanyoni) is a boundary-pushing South African artist, composer, producer, and creative entrepreneur from Mabopane, Pretoria. A self-taught pianist who began his professional journey in 2019, he has grown into a versatile musician working across sound design, music production, songwriting, vocal performance, and bandleading. He blends South African jazz, traditional melodies, and electronic textures into a sound that is both rooted and exploratory.

Through his work, he collaborates with diverse artists and communities, building platforms that preserve live music while nurturing emerging talent. His projects, including the Indoda EP and collaborations with international and local creatives, reflect a deep commitment to artistic innovation, cultural storytelling, and community development.

His work explores music as a living, evolving language of identity, memory, and imagination blending South African jazz, traditional rhythms, electronic textures, and improvisation to create music that feels both personal and collective. Beyond the stage and studio, he builds ecosystems of creativity through community concerts and collaborative projects, creating spaces where artists and audiences meet, exchange energy, and participate in the preservation and evolution of live music culture.

Ultimately, his work is about connection between tradition and innovation, the personal and the collective, the local and the global.

The DJ Concept

Afro Groove System

Bongani Givethanks' relationship with South African music goes beyond influence. It is rooted in curiosity, study, and deep listening. An intentional exploration of the country's musical history across generations, movements, cities, political eras, and sonic languages. From afro jazz, mbaqanga, and kwela to kwaito, house, gospel, and beyond, he traces the full spectrum of a nation's sound.

This journey gave rise to Imbali Sound Archive which is a digital platform for collaborative music research, documentation, and preservation. A notebook made public. A study turned into a shared resource.

From that foundation, Afro Groove System was born. It is his DJ alter ego, the sonic embodiment of the research. If the iMbali Sound Archive is the library, Afro Groove System is the listening session. Each DJ set becomes an output of study, inviting listeners into the process and not just the performance.

This is research in rhythm. This is history in motion. This is an open invitation to journey through South African music, from its roots to its echoes across the African diaspora and the world.

Digging deeper. Honouring lineage. Listening with intention.
